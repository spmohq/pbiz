The 7 headline formulas that make Medium articles using this structure get dramatically more clicks and reads: Headlines are extremely important on Medium because readers usually decide in 2–3 seconds whether to click your article. For articles that aim to sell a product through education, the headline must do three things: • attract attention • promise a useful insight • hint at a practical solution Professional writers often rely on repeatable headline frameworks rather than inventing headlines randomly. Below are 7 high-performing headline formulas commonly used for Medium-style educational articles. --- 1. The Problem–Solution Headline This is one of the most reliable formats. Structure: Problem + Clear Solution Example pattern: How to Solve [Specific Problem] Without [Common Frustration] Why it works: • Readers immediately recognize their problem • The promise of a solution is clear Best used when the problem is very specific and relatable. --- 2. The Mistake Headline People are naturally curious about mistakes. Structure: Number + Mistakes + Audience Example pattern: X Mistakes That Keep People From [Desired Outcome] Why it works: • Creates curiosity • Suggests hidden insight Readers click because they want to know: > “Am I making this mistake?” --- 3. The Contrarian Headline This headline challenges common beliefs. Structure: Why Something Popular Is Wrong Example pattern: Why Most Advice About [Topic] Doesn’t Work Why it works: • Disrupts expectations • creates curiosity This works especially well with your article structure because it connects directly to the section: “Why Most Advice Fails.” --- 4. The Framework Headline This headline introduces a method or system. Structure: The [Name] Framework for Achieving [Outcome] Example pattern: The Simple System That Helps You [Achieve Result] Why it works: • readers love structured methods • frameworks feel practical and teachable This headline works particularly well when your article introduces a named framework. --- 5. The Insight Headline This headline highlights a surprising realization. Structure: The Hidden Reason Behind a Problem Example pattern: The Real Reason People Struggle With [Topic] Why it works: • triggers curiosity • promises deeper understanding Readers feel they will learn something they didn’t know before. --- 6. The “Nobody Tells You” Headline This headline signals insider knowledge. Structure: What Nobody Tells You About [Topic] Example pattern: What Nobody Tells You About Learning [Skill] Why it works: • implies hidden knowledge • suggests the reader will gain an advantage This format performs well for educational content. --- 7. The Step-Based Headline This headline emphasizes practicality. Structure: Number + Steps + Desired Outcome Example pattern: X Steps to [Achieve Specific Result] Why it works: • clear expectation • structured promise • easy to understand This format works well when your article contains a step-by-step framework. --- How to Choose the Best Headline Before selecting a headline, ask three questions: 1. Is the problem recognizable? If yes → use a *Problem–Solution* headline. 2. Does the article challenge common beliefs? If yes → use a *Contrarian* headline. 3. Does the article introduce a method or system? If yes → use a *Framework* headline. --- One Important Medium Strategy The most effective headlines on Medium usually combine two elements: • curiosity • usefulness Example structure: Curiosity + Practical Outcome This combination attracts clicks without sounding like clickbait. --- Also add a DISCLOSURE DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENT Include the following disclosure naturally within the article (preferably at the end after the CTA of the article in a transparent and professional tone): Disclosure: This article may contain affiliate links. If you purchase through these links, I may earn a commission at no additional cost to you. The disclosure must feel transparent, calm, and compliant with Medium guidelines. It must not feel defensive or promotional.
